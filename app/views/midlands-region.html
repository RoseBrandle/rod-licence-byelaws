1. Overview of Severn Trent (Midlands) Byelaws

These byelaws for Severn Trent (Midlands) Region may apply to local waters or to whole river catchments areas - for example the River Severn and all its many tributaries.

These byelaws apply to anyone fishing with a rod and line for salmon, trout, coarse fish, eels, smelt and lamprey in all freshwater fisheries and tidal estuaries.

Separate byelaws apply to the River Wye, which are regulated by Natural Resources Wales.

Private fisheries may enforce their own additional rules, but national and regional byelaws still apply.

2. When and where you can fish

National coarse fishing close season rules apply across the Severn Trent (Midlands) region.

Salmon

You cannot fish for salmon and migratory trout (in any waters) from 8 October to 31 January.

You can fish for salmon from 1 February - 15 June with these restrictions:

  • You may only use an artificial or natural fly
  • You may not use a float
  • You may not remove any salmon (alive or dead)
  • You must return all fish to the water unharmed

Trout

You cannot fish for brown trout and rainbow trout in rivers, and for brown trout in unenclosed stillwaters, from 8 October to 17 March.

Fishing near weirs

You must not take, or attempt to take, fish by rod and line within a distance above or upstream of the crests, or below or downstream of the toe of these weirs:

  • 15 metres above and 45 metres below Shrewsbury Weir on the River Severn SJ501131
  • 15 metres above and 25 metres below Powick Weir on the River Teme SO832524, and Carreghofa Weir on the River Tanat SJ254219
  • 5 metres above and 15 metres below Tewkesbury Weir SO882328, Maisemore Weir SO818217 and Llanthony Weir SO822182 on the River Severn
  • 15 metres above and 35 metres below Diglis Weir on the River Severn SO847535

Stillwaters in the Severn Trent (Midlands) region designated as Sites of Special Scientific Interest (SSSI)

These are stillwaters which retain a close season.

3. Legal fishing tackle, lures and bait

National rules apply in the Severn Trent (Midlands) region to the number of rods you can use.

Lures, baits and weights

You must not use any form of lead weight attached to a fishing line other than those of 0.06 grams (size No. 14 to No. 8), or less, or of more than 28.35 grams (1 ounce).

"Lead weight" does not include lead incorporated into a weighted line, swim-feeder or fishing fly or lure.

Where the coarse fish close season applies and you are fishing for eels, trout or salmon, you must not use any float or bait (including ground bait) other than artificial or natural fly (excluding bloodworms, jokers and other midge larvae), spinners, minnows, worms, prawns or shrimps.

Keepnets and landing nets

You must not use a keepnet or keepsack in waters where the close season for coarse fish applies (15 March to 15 June).

You must not keep salmon (including salmon parr) or trout in a keepnet or keepsack.

Eels

In addition to the National Rules on fishing for eels, if you fish for eels during the coarse fish close season, you must not use a hook with a gape of less than 0.5 inches (12.7mm).

You may use either artificial or natural fly, spinners, minnows, worms, prawns or shrimps as bait, but not bloodworms, jokers and other midge larvae.

You may keep an eel in a keepnet or keep sack, but not during the coarse fish close season. Eels must be returned to water from which they were caught.

4. Catch limits, size limits, catch returns

For salmon and migratory trout catch limits, national byelaws apply.

Trout

You must not take or kill trout under these size limits measured from the tip of the nose or snout to the fork or cleft of the tail:

  • All trout (except Rainbow trout) – up to 20cm in all regional waters
  • Rainbow trout – up to 20cm - caught in the waters of the Rivers Derwent and Amber, including their tributaries, which are:

Above or upstream of their confluence at Ambergate, Derbyshire - SK346515 - excluding the stretch of the River Wye from Blackwell Mill near Buxton to Cressbrook Mill - SK173727.

Above or upstream of Ashford-in-the-Water - excluding any reservoir or lake formed by the construction of a dam across the valley of those rivers or across one of their tributaries.

If you catch any immature or undersized fish unintentionally, you must return it immediately to the same water with as little injury as possible.

Coarse (freshwater) fish

National rules apply across the Midlands region.

All lengths are measured from the tip of the snout to the fork of the tail.
