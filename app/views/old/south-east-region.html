1. Overview of South East Byelaws

These byelaws for the South East Region apply to anyone fishing with a rod and line for salmon, trout, coarse fish, eels, smelt and lamprey in all freshwater fisheries.

Within the South East Region there are rules that apply to:

  • The 'Thames Byelaw Area', which include the catchments of the Rivers Darent and Cray
  • Southern Byelaw Area - see the map below

Private fisheries may enforce their own additional rules, but national and regional byelaws still apply.

2. When and where you can fish

Regional close season rules apply across both the Thames and Southern areas. These are:

Close Season - Thames Byelaw Area

  • Coarse fishing in rivers, streams, drains: national byelaws apply
  • Eels in rivers, streams, drains: national byelaws apply, but there is no close season on the tidal River Thames downstream of the Thames Barrier
  • Brown and Rainbow Trout in rivers, streams, drains: 1st October to 31st March
  • Brown trout in fully enclosed waters: no close season
  • Rainbow trout in all stillwaters: no close season
  • Salmon and Migratory Trout in all waters: 1st October to 31st March

Close Season - Southern Byelaw Area

  • Coarse fishing in rivers, streams, drains: national byelaws apply
  • Eels in rivers, streams, drains: national byelaws apply
  • Brown and Rainbow trout in rivers, streams, drains: 1st November to 2nd April
  • Brown trout in fully enclosed waters: no close season
  • Rainbow trout in all stillwaters: no close season
  • Salmon and Migratory Trout in all waters: 1st November to 30th April

Canals and stillwaters retaining a close season

A small number of canals in the South East region are subject to a close season (15 March to 15 June). These are:

  • Kennet and Avon Canal, downstream of its confluence with the River Kennet at Kintbury
  • Lee Navigation, upstream of Aqueduct Lock
  • Two designated stretches of the Basingstoke Canal, from SU719514 to SU967575, and TQ016597 to TQ046616.
  • The Wey Navigation is classed as a river and therefore is subject to a close season

Sites of Special Scientific Interest (SSSI)

Some stillwaters have been designated 'Sites of Special Scientific Interest' (SSSI) and angling at these waters is prohibited during the close season.

When and where can I fish for eel?

National byelaws apply in the South East region

In the Thames byelaw area there is no close season when fishing for eels with rod and line in the tidal River Thames downstream of the Thames Barrier.

When can I fish for brown and rainbow trout?

The close season in all rivers, and for brown trout in unenclosed stillwaters, is the period from:

  • 1 October to 31 March within the Thames Byelaw Area and
  • 1 November to 2 April (dates inclusive) within the Southern byelaw area.

When can I fish for migratory trout?

The close season for migratory trout in all waters is the period 1 October to the 31 March in the Thames byelaw area and 1 November to the 30 April in the Southern Byelaw Area.

When can I fish for salmon?

The close season for salmon in all waters is the period 1 October to the 31 March in the Thames Byelaw Area and 3 October to the 16 January in the Southern Byelaw Area.

In the Thames Byelaw Area when fishing for salmon, trout, or rainbow trout during the annual close season for coarse fish - 15 March to 15 June dates - you may only fish with an artificial fly or lure.

In the Southern Byelaw Area during the annual close season for coarse fish - 15 March to 15 June - no person shall fish with rod and line for salmon, migratory trout or non-migratory trout (including rainbow trout), other than with artificial fly or lure.

3. Legal fishing tackle, lures and bait

Lures and baits

In the Thames Byelaw Area you must not fish with any form of gorge bait.

A gorge bait is a piece of stick, strip of plastic or bone hidden in the bait parallel to the line. When swallowed a tug on the line causes the gorge to turn sideways in the fish's throat.

Lead weight

You must not use any form of lead weight attached to a fishing line other than those of 0.06 grams (size No. 8), or less, or of more than 28.35 grams (1 ounce).

"Lead weight" does not include lead incorporated into a weighted line, swim-feeder or fishing fly or lure.

Hooks

In the Thames Byelaw Area you must not use more than three separate hooks attached to the same rod and line.

Rods

Rules around the number of rods permitted, use of landing nets, keepnets, gaffs or tailers are all covered in the National Byelaws.

4. Catch limits, size limits, catch returns

Salmon and migratory trout - catch limits

In the Thames Byelaw Area after the 16 June you must not take any more than two salmon a day from rivers, streams, drains and canals.

Coarse fish and eels - catch limits

Across the South East region, national byelaws apply.

Size limits - trout

You must not take any trout in the Thames Byelaw Area that are less than 25cm, or migratory trout less than 38cm.

In the Southern Byelaw Area you must not take any trout less than 23cm, and migratory trout less than 38cm.

The size is measured from the tip of the snout to the fork or cleft of the tail when the fish is laid flat.
