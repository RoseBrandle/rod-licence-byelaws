1. Overview of North East Byelaws

These byelaws for the North East Region apply to anyone fishing with a rod and line for salmon, trout, coarse fish, eels, smelt and lamprey in all freshwater fisheries.

Within the North East Region there are rules that apply to:

  • The entire North East Region
  • The Yorkshire Byelaw Area only
  • The Northumbria Byelaw Area only - see the map below

Private fisheries may enforce their own additional rules, but national and regional byelaws still apply.

2. When and where you can fish

Byelaws covering when and where you can fish across the entire North East Region are listed first in this section.

Rules specific to the Northumbria Byelaw Region and the the Yorkshire Byelaw Region are listed beneath.

Canals and stillwaters retaining a close season

One stretch of canal in the North East Region is subject to a close season (15 March to 15 June). This is:

  • The Aire and Calder Navigation between Castleford and Ferrybridge lock

There is no statutory close season on any other canal, reservoir, lake or pond for coarse fish.

Fishing near weirs and other obstructions

No fishing is allowed within a distance of 25 metres upstream and 95 metres downstream of the following structures, at the dates and times specified:

At all times

  • Framwellgate Dam - River Wear
  • Ferens Dam - River Gaunless
  • Tees Barrage - River Tees
  • Broken Scar - River Tees
  • Keilder Dam (downstream only) - River North Tyne
  • Ruswarp Weir - River Esk (to downstream side of Ruswarp road bridge

At night

  • Guyzance Mill - River Coquet
  • Pauperhaugh Bridge - River Coquet
  • Wylam Bridge - River Tyne
  • Dales House Weir - Dale House Beck

At night, or any time during September, October and November

  • On the River Aln: Lesbury Weir, Denwick bottom Dam, Denwick Dam, Cannongate Dam, Filberthaugh Weir, Hulne Park Weir
  • On the River Coquet: CoquetLodge Dam, Acklington Dam, Felton
  • On the River Blyth: Humford Dam
  • On the River Wansbeck: Bothal Dam, Sheepwash Weir, Morpeth East Mill, Highford Dam, Olives Mill
  • On the River Tyne: Riding Mill Weir, Hexham Bridge Weir, Devils Water Dam, Derwenthaugh Weir, Haltwhistle Weir, Featherstone Weir, Haydon Bridge Weir, Catton Bridge, Whitfield Dam, Chollerford Weir, Otterburn Mill Weir, Tarret Burn Burnmouth Falls, Esp Mill Falls, Roses Bower Falls
  • On the River Wear: Prebends, Chester-le-Street Weir, Burn Hall Weir, Wallnook Dam, Black Ling Hole, Bedburn Gauging Weir, Jocks Bridge Weir
  • On the Killhope Burn (tributary of the River Wear): Burtree Falls
  • On the River Tees: Low Force (Holwick Falls), High Force, Bowes Falls, Bowlees Falls

Fishing at night

Fishing in rivers, streams and drains for salmon or migratory trout between the end of the first hour after sunset and the beginning of the first hour before sunrise in not permitted unless the bait used consists of a natural or artificial fly with each hook not exceeding 10mm in diameter.

Fishing for eel

National byelaws for eel and shad apply in the North East Region.

Northumbria Byelaw Region

These byelaws apply on all waters in the Region from and including the River Tees northwards (not including the River Tweed or any of its tributaries).

Salmon and trout close seasons - Northumbria

  • The close season for salmon: 1 November to 31 January
  • The close season for all non-migratory trout on rivers, streams, drains and canals, and for brown trout on all waters other than enclosed stillwaters: 1 October to 21 March -
  • The close season for brown trout on Kielder Water, Broomlee Lough, the Derwent Reservoir and East and West Hallington reservoirs is: 1 November to 30 April
  • There is no close season for rainbow trout on any stillwater or for brown trout on enclosed stillwaters.

Yorkshire Byelaw Region

These byelaws apply on all waters within the Region southwards of (but not including) the River Tees and its tributaries.

Salmon and trout close seasons - Yorkshire

  • The close season for salmon: 1 November to 5 April
  • The close season for all non-migratory trout in rivers, streams, drains and canals and for brown trout in all waters other than enclosed stillwaters: 1 October to 24 March
  • There is no close season for rainbow trout in any stillwater or for brown trout on enclosed stillwaters

River Esk conservation area

Fishing with a rod and line for salmon or migratory trout is prohibited in the River Esk between Ruswarp Weir and a line drawn due west across the river from the southern-most end of Eskside Wharfe in Whitby.

Fishing with a rod and line for any kind of fish is prohibited in this area from 8pm or one hour after sunset (whichever is earlier) and 6am the following morning.

3. Legal fishing tackle, lures and bait

Weights, lures and baits

National byelaws on lead weights apply in this region.

On waters where the close season for freshwater fish applies, the only lures or baits which may be used during the close season are natural or artificial fly, minnow, worm, shrimp, prawn, sand eel or artificial lures.

It is an offence to fish for salmon using anything other than an artificial fly or artificial lure before 16 June.

When using live bait, National Byelaws apply.

Rods and nets

Rules around the number of rods permitted, use of landing nets, keepnets, gaffs or tailers are all covered by National Byelaws, with this exception:

Keepnet rule - Yorkshire Byelaw Region

On waters where there is a close season for coarse fish, keepnets cannot be used during that period.

4. Catch limits, size limits, catch returns

National Byelaws on catch limits and size limits apply across the North East region with the following exceptions:

Size limits - Yorkshire Byelaw Region

It is illegal to take trout less than 23cm in length

Catch limits - Yorkshire Byelaw Region

Not more than six trout may be taken away in one day without the written consent of the owner or occupier of the fishery.

Day or season tickets sometimes state higher catch limits and this may be taken as written consent. Any lower limits on a ticket must be observed.

Size limits - Northumbria Byelaw Region

It is illegal to take fish less than the size limits listed below:

  • Salmon: 30 cm
  • Migratory trout: 25.4cm

And for non-migratory trout:

  • River Tees below Egglestone Abbey Bridge: 22.5cm
  • River Wear below Harelaw Bridge: 22.5cm
  • River Coquet below Pauperhaugh Bridge: 22.5cm
  • River Tyne below Hexham Bridge: 22.5cm
  • River Tees and tributaries above High Force: 17.5cm
  • River Lune and tributaries above Selset Reservoir: 17.5cm
  • River Balder and tributaries above Balderhead Reservoir: 17.5cm
  • Kilton Beck and tributaries above Lockwood Beck Reservoir: 17.5cm
  • Burnhope Burn and tributaries above Burnhope Reservoir: 17.5cm
  • Waskerley Beck and tributaries above Tunstall Reservoir: 17.5cm
  • Any other waters covered by Northumbria byelaws: 20cm

Fish are measured from the tip of the snout to the fork of the tail. Undersized fish must be returned to the water immediately.
